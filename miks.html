Miks

Agent 30 — 7th Controller From Croatia Controller Medium Difficulty

Miks is Valorant's 30th agent and 7th Controller, hailing from Croatia. Revealed at VCT Masters Santiago 2026 and released March 18, 2026 (Patch 12.05, Season 2026 Act 2). His sonic abilities turn the battlefield into a concert — using sound waves for zone control, team healing, and crowd control. Miks brings something no other Controller has: direct team sustain.

Why Miks on Mobile?

  • Only Controller with team healing — Harmonize makes him invaluable in solo queue where you can't rely on teammates to stay alive
  • Sonic visual effects are easy to track — Wave animations and pulse rings are highly visible on mobile screens
  • Smoke placement is forgiving — Tap-to-place controls make Controller gameplay more accessible on mobile than on PC
  • Bassquake ultimate is large and impactful — Big AoE zones are easier to use effectively on mobile with tap controls
  • Utility-first playstyle — Even with poor aim, Miks provides enormous team value through heals and zone control

Skills on Mobile

M-Pulse (C) — Sonic Pulse

Cheap utility. Throws a sonic pulse that briefly denies vision in the area. Great for site takes or retakes. Assign to right thumb for fast access during fights.

Harmonize (Q) — Team Heal/Buff

Miks' signature support ability. Heals and buffs nearby teammates. On mobile, pre-cast before pushing with your team. Extremely valuable in solo queue.

Waveform (E) — Signature Smoke/Zone

Your main smoke ability. Deploys a sonic wave barrier that blocks vision and damages enemies who push through. Place it between chokepoints for maximum value.

Bassquake (X) — Ultimate

A massive AoE sonic zone. Deals damage and applies a strong slow to enemies inside. Drop it on bomb sites during retakes or site takes for devastating control.

Mobile-Specific Tips

  • Heal before you frag — Harmonize is most valuable early in fights. Cast it as your team commits to a push
  • Smoke timing is everything — Waveform placement matters less than timing. Cast as enemies are rotating, not when they're already in position
  • Pair Bassquake with Sage wall — Sonic zone + Sage slow = near-impossible for enemies to retake
  • M-Pulse for intel — If you're not sure if a corner is held, M-Pulse is cheap enough to check safely
  • Harmonize has range limit — Stay close to your team. On mobile, the mini-map helps you track teammate positions
  • Anchor with smokes on defense — Place Waveform at chokepoints and fall back; let the sonic pulse do the work

When to Pick Miks

Miks is strongest in solo queue where team coordination is unreliable. His healing ability compensates for teammates who can't self-sustain. He's ideal on defensive rounds where Harmonize can sustain multiple pushes, and on attacking rounds where his M-Pulse + Waveform combo can open sites cheaply. He's weaker when you have a coordinated team with a dedicated Sage — in those cases, a more damage-focused Controller may add more value.
