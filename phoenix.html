Phoenix

DuelistMedium Difficulty

Phoenix is a self-sustaining duelist who thrives on aggression. Unlike Jett, Phoenix's abilities return to him after use — making him budget-friendly and aggressive-friendly on mobile where economy matters.

Why Phoenix?

  • Free abilities — Your flashes and walls return if they don't hit anything. Play aggressive without wasting credits
  • Self-heal — Hot Hands is your get-out-of-jail card in close-range fights
  • Mobile-friendly flashes — Curveball is point-and-throw, easier than Jett's instant smoke on mobile
  • Entry potential — Blind enemies with flash, push with Hot Hands active

Skills on Mobile

Hot Hands (C) — Fireball

Throw it at your feet when pushed. The AoE damages enemies and heals you. On mobile, assign to a quick-access button.

Curveball (Q) — Flash

Your entry tool. Flash corners you're about to swing. On mobile, hold and drag to curve the throw.

Blaze (E) — Wall

Blocks sight and damages enemies who push through. Place it to cut site in half on attack.

Run it Back (X) — Ultimate

Go for aggressive plays knowing you respawn at your marker. On mobile, pre-mark a safe position before ulting.

Mobile Tips

  • Hot Hands usage — Don't save it. If you're in a close-range fight, throw it at your feet immediately
  • Flash timing — Your flash blind duration is ~1.8s. Count it and swing at 1.5s for maximum surprise
  • Wall blocks healing — Use Blaze wall to block enemy healing in post-plant situations
  • Marker placement — Before using ult, tap and hold to place your respawn marker in a safe spot

⚙️ Recommended Settings

DPI400-500

When to Pick Phoenix

Phoenix is excellent on tight maps like Ascent and Bind where his self-sustain and flashing shine. He's the best starter duelist on mobile because he doesn't punish you for missing ability timing.
