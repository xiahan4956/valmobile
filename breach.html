Breach

InitiatorHard Difficulty

Breach is the flash-and-destroy initiator. His abilities pierce walls and stun enemies, making him essential for aggressive site takes. On mobile, his difficulty is higher due to the need for precise multi-point casting.

Why Breach on Mobile?

  • Wall-flashing — Flashpoint and Aftershock pierce walls. Get kills through cover
  • Stunning power — Fault Line is one of the longest stuns in the game
  • Site-taking synergy — Flash enemies off angles, then Breach pushes through
  • Pairing potential — Best paired with Raze or Jett for guaranteed damage follow-up

Skills on Mobile

Flashpoint (C)

Blinds enemies in a cone. On mobile, hold and drag to aim through walls before releasing.

Fault Line (Q)

Long-range stun. The delay makes it tricky on mobile — pre-cast before enemies peek.

Aftershock (E)

Damage through walls. Use it to flush enemies from behind cover during site takes.

Rolling Thunder (X)

Multi-point stun. On mobile, it covers a large area — use it on site or before pushing chokepoints.

Mobile Tips

  • Fault Line delay — There's a ~0.5s delay before the line fires. Fire it before enemies peek, not when they're already peeking
  • Flashpoint aiming — This is the hardest ability on mobile. Practice the drag-to-aim gesture in the range
  • Use Aftershock post-plant — It damages through walls, making it perfect for post-plant denial
  • Pair aggressively — Breach needs teammates to follow up. Don't use him in solo-queue without coordination

When to Pick Breach

Best on tight maps (Bind, Haven) where his multi-point stuns cover multiple angles. He's weak in solo-queue because his abilities need teammates to capitalize. Only pick him if you're playing with a duo or stack that can follow up on your flashes.
