Viper

ControllerMedium Difficulty

Viper is the map-control controller who trades smoke uptime for area denial. Her chemical cloud blocks vision and damages — making her the best agent for locking down sites post-plant.

Why Viper on Mobile?

  • Post-plant queen — Her smoke + damage combo forces enemies off defuse
  • One-way smokes — Toxic Screen creates one-way walls that enemies can't see through
  • Area denial — Snake Bite damages through walls on post-plant scenarios
  • Low team dependency — Strong solo impact, doesn't need coordinated teammates

Skills on Mobile

Snake Bite (C)

Damage + debuff field. On mobile, pre-place near defuse spots for maximum post-plant denial.

Poison Cloud (Q)

Gas orb that deploys a lingering cloud. Can be picked up and reused. On mobile, throw it to block off rotation paths.

Toxic Screen (E)

Wall that blocks vision one-way. On mobile, the wall has a short activation delay — place it before peeking.

Viper's Pit (X)

Large damage zone where you're immune. On mobile, activate it when 2+ enemies cluster near your position.

Mobile Tips

  • Smoke management — Unlike other controllers, Viper's "smokes" cost health. Watch your toxin meter and retreat to regenerate
  • One-way wall — Position yourself on the side where the wall is thin — enemies can't see through but you can
  • Pick up orbs — Always retrieve Poison Cloud orbs after rounds. They recharge your smoke fuel
  • Ulting at good spots — Viper's Pit on site is devastating. Get on site, pop ult, force enemies into it

When to Pick Viper

Best on maps with long sightlines (Ascent, Icebox) where her one-way walls shine. She's weaker on maps like Bind where her mobility is a liability. On mobile, her learning curve is steeper than Brimstone but her ceiling is higher.
